Compliance Corner

AB 723 reminder — disclosing digitally altered images

Effective January 1, 2026, California Business & Professions Code §10140.8 requires disclosure whenever listing images have been digitally altered. SDMLS Participants, Subscribers, and Vendors are all responsible for compliance.

What counts as "digitally altered"

A Quick Refresher

  • "Digitally altered" goes beyond color correction. Virtual staging, removed objects, edited features, and AI-generated enhancements all qualify.
  • Disclosure must travel with the image — wherever it appears, on the MLS and downstream.
  • When in doubt, disclose. The safer call is always disclosure; reach out to support if a specific case is unclear.

Full SDMLS guidance is at sdmls.com/ab-723.

IDX: the case for owning your digital storefront

When a buyer encounters one of your listings online, they're more likely to see it first on a national portal than on your own site. Internet Data Exchange (IDX) changes that — letting you display active MLS listings directly on your site, with the lead and the brand impression staying with you.

Three data feeds · why iHomefinder fits · how to start

Three Data Feeds, Three Purposes

  • IDX — public-facing display of active listings on agent and broker websites.
  • VOW (Virtual Office Website) — lets registered users on your site access additional MLS data after signing in.
  • BBO (Broker Back-Office) — full MLS data access for brokers running internal analytics and operations tools.

The strategic value: fresh, auto-updating content that signals authority to search engines; behavioral data on what each visitor is actually searching; direct lead capture tied to your CRM; and brand consistency — your site looks like you, not like a portal.

Where iHomefinder fits

SDMLS lists two approved IDX partners: iHomefinder and Luxury Presence. iHomefinder combines IDX property search, lead capture, and a real estate CRM into a single connected platform — when a buyer saves a property on your site, that activity flows directly to your contact record.

What's in the iHomefinder Toolkit

  • Drop-in integration with WordPress, Wix, Squarespace, or custom sites.
  • Lead capture with automated property alerts, saved searches, and behavior tracking.
  • Integrated CRM so search activity, listing views, and lead profiles live in one place.
  • Marketing automation — drip campaigns, listing alerts, and market updates triggered by lead behavior.
  • AI-assisted content for emails and posts, powered by your MLS data.

Start at sdmls.com/members/idx to compare approved partners and learn more about IDX, VOW, and BBO access.

What the numbers say

Detached is the story this month: median up 2.5% from March and 4.3% year-over-year, closed sales jumping from 1,335 to 1,498, and new listings finally responding to demand. Homes are still selling in 12 days at 100% of list — a clean seller's market accelerating into spring.

Attached is steadier. The median ticked up only marginally and remains essentially flat year-over-year. The more telling number is $/sq ft at $638, still ~5% below April 2025's $670 — a reminder that buyers in this segment are pricing for value, with HOA dues, special assessments, and lending standards on non-warrantable projects all weighing on appetite.
